    JULIA LETLOW, Republican, of Start, LA; born in Monroe, LA, March 
16, 1981; education: B.A., University of Louisiana, Monroe, LA, 2002; 
M.A., University of Louisiana, Monroe, 2005; Ph.D., University of South 
Florida, Tampa, FL, 2011; professional: ombudsperson and special 
projects coordinator, University of Louisiana, Monroe, LA; director, 
education, resident patient safety, and quality improvement and clinical 
instructor, Tulane University, New Orleans, LA; director, marketing and 
communications, University of Louisiana, Monroe, LA; executive director, 
external affairs and strategic communications, University of Louisiana, 
Monroe, LA, 2018-19; executive assistant to president for external 
affairs and community outreach, University of Louisiana, Monroe, LA; 
religion: Presbyterian; family: two children; committees: 
Appropriations; elected to the 117th Congress, by special election, on 
March 20, 2021, to fill the vacancy caused by the death of U.S. 
Representative-elect Luke J. Letlow.
