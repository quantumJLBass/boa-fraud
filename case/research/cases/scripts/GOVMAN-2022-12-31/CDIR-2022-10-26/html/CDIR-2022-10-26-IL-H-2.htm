    ROBIN L. KELLY, Democrat, of Matteson, IL; born in New York, NY, 
April 30, 1956; education: B.A., psychology, Bradley University, Peoria, 
IL, 1977; M.A., counseling, Bradley University, 1982; Ph.D., political 
science, Northern Illinois University, DeKalb, IL, 2004; professional: 
counselor; community affairs director, Matteson, IL, 1992-2006; member, 
Illinois State House of Representatives, 2003-07; Chief of Staff, 
Illinois State Treasurer, 2007-10; Chief Administrative Officer, Cook 
County, IL, 2010-12; married: Dr. Nathaniel Horn; two children; 
caucuses: co-chair, Congressional Caucus on Black Women and Girls; co-
chair, Diversifying Tech Caucus; co-chair, Gun Violence Prevention Task 
Force; Congressional Black Caucus; committees: Energy and Commerce; 
Oversight and Reform; elected to the 113th Congress on April 9, 2013, by 
special election, to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of U.S. 
Representative Jesse L. Jackson, Jr.; reelected to each succeeding 
Congress.
