    LUCILLE ROYBAL-ALLARD, Democrat, of Los Angeles, CA; born in Los 
Angeles, June 12, 1941; education: B.A., California State University, 
Los Angeles, CA, 1965; professional: served in the California State 
Assembly, 1987-92; the first woman to serve as the chair of the 
California Democratic Congressional Delegation, 105th Congress; the 
first woman to chair the Congressional Hispanic Caucus, 106th Congress; 
the first Latina appointed to the House Appropriations Committee, 106th 
Congress; the first Latina ranking member of a House Appropriations 
subcommittee, 114th Congress; the first Latina chairwoman of a House 
Appropriations subcommittee, 116th Congress; appointed to the 
Smithsonian Institution Board of Regents in 2019; married: Edward T. 
Allard III; two children: Lisa Marie and Ricardo; two stepchildren: 
Angela and Guy Mark; committees: Appropriations; elected to the 103rd 
Congress on November 3, 1992; the first Mexican-American woman elected 
to Congress; reelected to each succeeding Congress.
