    RON WYDEN, Democrat, of Portland, OR; born in Wichita, KS, May 3, 
1949; education: graduated from Palo Alto High School, 1967; B.A. in 
political science, with distinction, Stanford University, Stanford, CA, 
1971; J.D., University of Oregon Law School, Eugene, OR, 1974; 
professional: attorney; member, American Bar Association; former 
director, Oregon Legal Services for the Elderly; former public member, 
Oregon State Board of Examiners of Nursing Home Administrators; 
cofounder and codirector, Oregon Gray Panthers, 1974-80; married: Nancy 
Bass Wyden; children: Adam David, Lilly Anne, Ava Rose, William Peter, 
and Scarlett Willa; committees: chair, Finance; vice chair, Joint 
Committee on Taxation; Budget; Energy and Natural Resources; Permanent 
Select Committee on Intelligence; elected to the 97th Congress on 
November 4, 1980; reelected to each succeeding Congress; elected to the 
U.S. Senate on February 6, 1996, to fill the unexpired term of Senator 
Bob Packwood; reelected to each succeeding Senate term.
