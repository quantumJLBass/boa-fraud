    TAMMY BALDWIN, Democrat, of Madison, WI; born in Madison, February 
11, 1962; education: graduated, Madison West High School, Madison, 1980; 
A.B., Smith College, Northampton, MA, 1984; J.D., University of 
Wisconsin Law School, Madison, WI, 1989; professional: appointed to 
Madison Common Council, Madison, WI, 1986; elected to Dane County Board 
of Supervisors, Madison, WI, 1986-94; state representative, Wisconsin 
State Assembly, Madison, WI, 1992-98; Democratic Conference Secretary, 
2017-present; committees: Appropriations; Commerce, Science, and 
Transportation; Health, Education, Labor, and Pensions; elected to the 
106th Congress on November 3, 1998; reelected to the succeeding six 
Congresses; elected to the U.S. Senate on November 6, 2012; reelected to 
the U.S. Senate on November 6, 2018.
