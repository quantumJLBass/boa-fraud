    RICK SCOTT, Republican, of Naples, FL; born in Bloomington, IL, 
December 1, 1952; education: B.S., business administration, University 
of Missouri-Kansas City, Kansas City, MO; J.D., Southern Methodist 
University, Dallas, TX; professional: attorney, Johnson & Swanson, 
Dallas, TX; founder of Columbia Hospital Corporation, the world's 
largest health care company; military: active duty, U.S. Navy radar man, 
USS Glover; public service: Governor, State of Florida, 2011-19; 
married: wife, Ann; children: two daughters, Allison and Jordan; six 
grandsons: Auguste, Quinton, Sebastian, Eli, Louie, and Jude; 
committees: Armed Services; Budget; Commerce, Science, and 
Transportation; Homeland Security and Governmental Affairs; Special 
Committee on Aging; elected to the U.S. Senate on November 6, 2018.
