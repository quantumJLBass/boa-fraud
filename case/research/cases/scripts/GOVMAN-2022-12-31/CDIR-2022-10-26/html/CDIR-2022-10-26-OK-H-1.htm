    KEVIN HERN, Republican, of Tulsa, OK; born in Belton, MO, December 
4, 1961; education: B.S., Arkansas Tech University, Russellville, AR, 
1986; M.B.A., University of Arkansas, Little Rock, AR, 1999; attended 
the Georgia Institute of Technology, studying for a Ph.D. in 
astronautical engineering; professional: McDonald's franchisee; 
chairman, Finance Committee,

[[Page 218]]

2011-15, Oklahoma Turnpike Authority, Oklahoma City, OK; religion: 
Protestant, member of the Church at Battle Creek; family: married to 
Tammy; three children; committees: Ways and Means; appointed to the 
115th Congress by the Governor of Oklahoma under the provisions of 
Oklahoma Statute, Section 26-12-101 (B) to fill the vacancy caused by 
the resignation of U.S. Representative Jim Bridenstine, and took the 
oath of office on November 13, 2018; elected to the 116th Congress on 
November 6, 2018; reelected to the 117th Congress on November 3, 2020.
