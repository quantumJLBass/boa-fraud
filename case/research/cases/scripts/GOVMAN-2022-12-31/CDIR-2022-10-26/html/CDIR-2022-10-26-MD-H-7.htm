    KWEISI MFUME, Democrat, of Baltimore, MD; born in Baltimore, 
October 24, 1948; education: attended Baltimore City Public Schools; 
B.S., magna cum laude, Morgan State University, Baltimore, 1976; M.A., 
Johns Hopkins University, Baltimore, 1984; member, Baltimore City 
Council, 1979-86; Chairman of the Board of Regents, Morgan State 
University; founding director, WEAA-FM Radio; host, ``The Remarkable 
Journey'' syndicated TV series, Hearst Broadcasting; member: Pi Sigma 
Alpha National Political Honor Society; NAACP; former executive 
director, National Medical Association; married: Tiffany McMillian; six 
sons: Donald, Kevin, Keith, Ronald, Michael, and Christopher; 
committees: Education and Labor; Oversight and Reform; Small Business; 
elected to the 100th Congress, November 4, 1986; reelected to the 101st 
through 104th Congresses; elected to the 116th Congress, by special 
election, on April 28, 2020, to fill the vacancy caused by the death of 
U.S. Representative Elijah E. Cummings; reelected to the 117th Congress 
on November 3, 2020.
