    BARBARA BOXER, Democrat, of Greenbrae, CA; born in Brooklyn, NY, 
November 11, 1940; education: B.A., economics, Brooklyn College, 1962; 
employment: stockbroker and economic researcher with securities firms on 
Wall Street, 1962-65; journalist and associate editor, Pacific Sun 
newspaper, 1972-74; congressional aide, Fifth Congressional District, 
California, 1974-76; elected Marin County Board of Supervisors, 1976-82; 
first woman president, Marin County Board of Supervisors; married: 
Stewart Boxer, 1962; children: Doug and Nicole; elected November 2, 1982 
to 98th Congress; reelected to the 99th-102nd Congresses; Senate 
committees: Commerce, Science and Transportation; Environment and Public 
Works; Foreign Relations; subcommittees: Communications; Consumer 
Affairs, Foreign Commerce and Tourism; Oceans, Atmosphere and Fisheries; 
Surface Transportation and Merchant Marine; chairman, Superfund, Toxics, 
Risk, and Waste Management; Transportation, Infrastructure and Nuclear 
Safety; African Affairs; Near Eastern and South Asian Affairs; chairman, 
International Operations and Terrorism; leadership positions: Chief 
Deputy for Strategic Outreach; Deputy Whip (Western Region); elected to 
the U.S. Senate on November 3, 1992; reelected to each succeeding Senate 
term.
