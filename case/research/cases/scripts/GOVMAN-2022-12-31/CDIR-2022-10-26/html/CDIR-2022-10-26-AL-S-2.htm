    TOMMY TUBERVILLE, Republican, of Auburn, AL; born in Camden, AR, 
September 18, 1954; education: B.S., physical education, Southern 
Arkansas University, Magnolia, AR, 1976; defensive back, Southern 
Arkansas University; professional: Hermitage High School assistant and 
head football coach, 1976-79; Arkansas State University, assistant 
football coach, 1980-84; University of Miami football assistant and 
defensive coordinator, 1986-93; Texas A&M defensive coordinator, 1994; 
University of Mississippi head football coach, 1995-98; Auburn 
University head football coach, 1999-2008; ESPN and ESPNU studio 
analyst, 2008; Texas Tech University head football coach, 2010-12; 
University of Cincinnati head football coach, 2013-16; ESPN, full-time 
broadcast analyst, 2017; honors: five national Coach of the Year awards; 
Arkansas Sports Hall of Fame inductee, 2008; religion: Christian; 
family: wife, Suzanne; children: Tucker and Troy; committees: 
Agriculture, Nutrition, and Forestry; Armed Services; Health, Education, 
Labor, and Pensions; Veterans' Affairs; elected to the 117th Congress on 
November 3, 2020.
