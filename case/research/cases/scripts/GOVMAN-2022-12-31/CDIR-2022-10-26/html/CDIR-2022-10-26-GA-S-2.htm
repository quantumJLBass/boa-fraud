    RAPHAEL G. WARNOCK, Democrat, of Atlanta, GA; born in Savannah, GA, 
July 23, 1969; education: B.A., psychology, Morehouse College, Atlanta, 
GA, 1991; M.Div., M.Phil., and Ph.D., Union Theological Seminary, New 
York, NY; professional: Baptist pastor; committees: Agriculture, 
Nutrition, and Forestry; Banking, Housing, and Urban Affairs; Commerce, 
Science, and Transportation; Joint Economic Committee; Special Committee 
on Aging; elected to the U.S. Senate on January 5, 2021, for the term 
ending January 3, 2023, in a special election runoff against appointed 
Senator Kelly Loeffler, to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of 
Senator Johnny Isakson, and took the oath of office on January 20, 2021.
