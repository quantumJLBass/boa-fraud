    ANN KIRKPATRICK, Democrat, of Tucson, AZ; born in McNary, AZ, March 
24, 1950; education: B.A., University of Arizona, Tucson, AZ, 1972; 
J.D., University of Arizona College of Law, Tucson, AZ, 1979; 
professional: Coconino Deputy County Attorney, 1980-81; Pima Deputy 
County Attorney, 1981-85; Sedona City Attorney, 1990-91; partner, 
Kirkpatrick & Harris Law Firm P.C., 1991-2008; instructor, Business Law 
& Ethics, Coconino Community College, 2004; Representative, Arizona 
State House, District 2, 2005-07; religion: Catholic; married: husband, 
Roger Curley; two children; caucuses: 4-H Caucus; Afterschool Caucus; 
Agritourism Caucus; Air Force Caucus; Army Caucus; Bipartisan 
Congressional Bus Caucus; Broadband Taskforce; Central America Caucus; 
Childhood Cancer Caucus; Congressional Brain Injury Task Force; 
Congressional Caucus to End the Youth Vaping Epidemic; Congressional 
National Parks Caucus; Dairy Farmers Caucus; Fire Services Caucus; 
Future of Transportation Caucus; General Aviation Caucus; House Food 
Recovery Caucus; I-11 Interstate Caucus; Labor Caucus; Medicare for All 
Caucus; Mom's Task Force on Family Separation; Name of Caucus / Task 
Force; NASA Caucus; New Dems; Pro-Choice Caucus; Refugee Caucus; Safe 
Climate

[[Page 12]]

Caucus; Small Brewers Caucus; Solar Caucus; Voting Rights Caucus; Women 
Veterans Task Force; committees: Appropriations; elected to the 111th 
Congress to represent the 1st District on November 4, 2008; elected to 
the 113th Congress to represent the 1st District on November 6, 2012; 
reelected to the 114th Congress on November 4, 2014; elected to 
represent the 2nd District in the 116th Congress on November 6, 2018; 
reelected to the 117th Congress on November 3, 2020.
