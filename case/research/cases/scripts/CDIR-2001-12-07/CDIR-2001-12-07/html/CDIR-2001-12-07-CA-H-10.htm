    ELLEN TAUSCHER, Democrat, of Pleasanton, CA; born in East Newark, 
NJ, November 15, 1951; graduated, Harrison High School, Harrison, NJ, 
1969; B.S., early childhood education, Seton Hall University, NJ; 
founder and CEO, The Registry Companies, first national child care 

[[Page 23]]

provider pre-employment screening service, 1992-present; one of the 
first women to hold a seat on the New York Stock Exchange (1977-79); 
Wall Street trader and investment banker, 1979-88; author of The Child 
Care Source Book; created the Tauscher Foundation, which has provided 
$150,000 to California and Texas elementary schools for purchase of 
computer equipment; member: NARAL, CARAL, Planned Parenthood, Seton Hall 
University Board of Regents; endorsed by Emily's List; cochair, Dianne 
Feinstein's 1992, and 1994, U.S. Senatorial campaigns; married William 
Y. Tauscher in 1989; one child: Katherine; committees: Armed Services; 
Transportation and Infrastructure; elected to the 105th Congress; 
reelected to each succeeding Congress.
