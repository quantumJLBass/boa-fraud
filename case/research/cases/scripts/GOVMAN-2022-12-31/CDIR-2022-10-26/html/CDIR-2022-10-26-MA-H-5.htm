    KATHERINE M. CLARK, Democrat, of Melrose, MA; born in New Haven, 
CT, July 17, 1963; education: B.A., St. Lawrence University, Canton, NY, 
1985; J.D., Cornell School of

[[Page 133]]

Law, Ithaca, NY, 1989; M.P.A., Harvard University, Cambridge, MA, 1997; 
professional: admitted to the Massachusetts Bar, 1997; served as general 
counsel for the Massachusetts Office of Child Care Services; Chief of 
the Policy Division for the Massachusetts Attorney General and 
prosecutor; elected in March 2008 to the Massachusetts House of 
Representatives; elected to the Massachusetts State Senate in November 
2010; leadership roles: vice chair of the Democratic Caucus for the 
116th Congress; assistant speaker of the House for the 117th Congress; 
religion: Protestant; married: Rodney Dowell; children: Addison, Riley, 
and Nathaniel; Democratic Steering and Policy Committee; committees: 
Appropriations; elected to the 113th Congress, by special election, on 
December 10, 2013; reelected to each succeeding Congress.
