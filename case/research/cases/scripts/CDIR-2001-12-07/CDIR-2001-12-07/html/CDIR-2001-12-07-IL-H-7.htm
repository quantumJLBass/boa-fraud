    DANNY K. DAVIS, Democrat, of Chicago, IL; born in Parkdale, AR, on 
September 6, 1941; B.A., Arkansas A.M. & N. College, 1961; M.A., Chicago 
State University; Ph.D., Union Institute, Cincinnati, OH; educator and 
health planner-administrator; board of directors, National Housing 
Partnership; Cook County Board of Commissioners, 1990-96; former 
alderman of the Chicago City Council's 29th ward, receiving the 
Independent Voters of Illinois ``Best Alderman Award'' for 1980-81, 
1981-82, and 1989-90; cochair, Clinton-Gore-Braun '92; founder and past 
president, Westside Association for Community Action; past president, 
National Association of Community Health Centers; 1987 recipient of the 
Leon M. Despres Award; married to Vera G. Davis; two sons: Jonathan and 
Stacey; committees: Government Reform; Small Business; elected to the 
105th Congress; reelected to each succeeding Congress.
