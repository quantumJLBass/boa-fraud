    KIM SCHRIER, M.D., Democrat, of Sammamish, WA; born in Los Angeles, 
CA, August 23, 1968; education: Phi Beta Kappa, B.A., astrophysics, 
University of California, Berkeley, CA, 1991; M.D., University of 
California Davis School of Medicine, Davis, CA, 1997; professional: 
pediatric residency, Lucile Packard Children's Hospital at Stanford 
University, Palo Alto, CA, 2000; pediatrician, Virginia Mason Medical 
Center, Issaquah, WA, 2001; awards: Best Pediatrician in the Greater 
Seattle Area, 2013, by Parents Map Magazine; religion: Jewish; family: 
married to David; one son: Sam; caucuses: New Democrat Coalition; 
committees: Agriculture; Energy and Commerce; elected to the 116th 
Congress on November 6, 2018, becoming the only woman doctor in Congress 
and the first pediatrician; reelected to the 117th Congress on November 
3, 2020.
