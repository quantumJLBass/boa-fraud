    CHRISTOPHER S. MURPHY, Democrat, of Wethersfield, CT; born in White 
Plains, Westchester County, NY, August 3, 1973; education: attended 
Exeter College, Oxford, England, 1994-95; graduated with honors with a 
double major in history and political science, Williams College, 
Williamstown, MA, 1996; J.D., University of Connecticut, Hartford, CT, 
2002; professional: lawyer, private practice; Southington, CT, planning 
and zoning commission, 1997-99; practiced real estate and banking law 
from 2002-06, with the firm of Ruben, Johnson & Morgan in Hartford; 
member of the Connecticut State House of Representatives, 1999-2003; 
member of the Connecticut State Senate, 2003-06; married: Cathy Holahan, 
a legal aid attorney who represents children in need in New Britain and 
Waterbury; children: Owen and Rider; committees: Appropriations; Foreign 
Relations; Health, Education, Labor, and Pensions; elected to the 110th 
Congress on November 7, 2006; reelected to the next two succeeding 
Congresses; elected to the U.S. Senate on November 6, 2012; reelected to 
the U.S. Senate on November 6, 2018.
