    CHRIS VAN HOLLEN, Democrat, of Kensington, MD; born in Karachi, 
Pakistan, January 10, 1959; education: B.A., Swarthmore College, 
Swarthmore, PA, 1982; master's in public policy, Harvard University, 
Cambridge, MA, 1985; J.D., Georgetown University, Washington, DC, 1990; 
professional: attorney; legislative assistant to former Senator Charles 
McC. Mathias, Jr., of Maryland; staff member, U.S. Senate Committee on 
Foreign Relations; senior legislative advisor to former Maryland 
Governor William Donald Schaefer; public service: elected, Maryland 
House of Delegates, 1990; elected, Maryland State Senate, 1994; married: 
Katherine; children: Anna, Nicholas, and Alexander; committees: 
Appropriations; Banking, Housing, and Urban Affairs; Budget; Foreign 
Relations; elected to the 108th Congress on November 5, 2002; reelected 
to each succeeding Congress; elected to the U.S. Senate on November 8, 
2016.
