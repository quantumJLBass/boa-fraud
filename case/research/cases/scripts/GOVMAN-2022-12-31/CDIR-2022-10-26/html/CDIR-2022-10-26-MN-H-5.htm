    ILHAN OMAR, Democrat, of Minneapolis, MN; born in Mogadishu, 
Somalia, October 4, 1982; education: B.A., political science and 
international studies, North Dakota State University, Fargo, ND, 2011; 
professional: community nutrition educator, University of Minnesota, 
Greater Minneapolis-St. Paul area, 2006-09; manager, Kari Dzeidzic's 
Campaign for Minnesota State Senate, 2012; child nutrition outreach 
coordinator, Minnesota State Department of Education, 2012-13; manager, 
Andrew Johnson's Campaign for Minneapolis City Council seat, 2013; 
senior policy aide, Office of Minneapolis City Councilman Andrew 
Johnson, 2013-15; member, District 60B and Assistant Minority Leader, 
Minnesota State House of Representatives, 2017-19; committees: Education 
and Labor; Foreign Affairs; elected to the 116th Congress on November 6, 
2018; reelected to the 117th Congress on November 3, 2020.
