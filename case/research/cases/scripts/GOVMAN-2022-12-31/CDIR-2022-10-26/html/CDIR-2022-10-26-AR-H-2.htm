    J. FRENCH HILL, Republican, of Little Rock, AR; born in Little 
Rock, December 5, 1956; education: B.S., economics,  magna cum laude, 
Vanderbilt University, Nashville, TN, 1979; professional: senior 
financial analyst, InterFirst Corporation, 1979-82; legislative 
assistant, assistant to the chairman, Subcommittee on Housing and Urban 
Development, the Honorable John Tower (R-TX), Senate Committee on 
Banking, Housing, and Urban Affairs, 1982-84; director, Mason Best 
Company, 1984-89; Deputy Assistant Secretary (Corporate Finance), U.S. 
Department of the Treasury, 1989-91; Special Assistant to the President, 
Executive Office of the White House, 1991-93; executive officer, Regions 
West, 1993-99; chief executive officer, Delta Trust & Banking Corp., 
1999-2014; serves on the Congressional Oversight Commission established 
by the CARES Act; religion: Roman Catholic; family: married, two 
children; caucuses: co-chair, Congressional Skilled American Workforce 
Caucus; vice co-chair, Congressional Historically Black Colleges and 
Universities (HBCU) Caucus; Congressional Air Force Caucus; 
Congressional Army Caucus; Congressional Battlefield Caucus; 
Congressional Bipartisan Congressional Arts Caucus; Congressional 
Boating Caucus; Congressional Caucus on Fitness; Congressional Caucus on 
Foster Youth; Congressional Caucus on India and Indian-Americans; 
Congressional Chicken Caucus; Congressional Congenital Heart Caucus; 
Congressional Diabetes Caucus; Congressional Dyslexia Caucus; 
Congressional French Caucus; Congressional Historic Preservation Caucus; 
Congressional House Republican Israel Caucus; Congressional House Small 
Brewers Caucus; Congressional Humanities Caucus; Congressional 
International Conservation Caucus; Congressional Israel Allies Caucus; 
Congressional Kidney Caucus; Congressional Missile Defense Caucus; 
Congressional National Guard and Reserve Components Caucus (NGRCC); 
Congressional Natural Gas Caucus; Congressional Prayer Caucus; 
Congressional Scouting Caucus; Congressional Solidarity Caucus; 
Congressional Sportsman's Caucus; Congressional STEAM Caucus; 
Congressional U.S.-Japan Caucus; Congressional Study Group on Japan; 
Congressional Wine Caucus; committees: Financial Services; elected to 
the 114th Congress on November 4, 2014; reelected to each succeeding 
Congress.
