    DIANE E. WATSON, Democrat, of Los Angeles, CA; education: Bachelor 
of Arts Degree in Education from the University of California, Los 
Angeles; Master of Science Degree in School Psychology from California 
State University, Los Angeles; attended the John F. Kennedy School of 
Government at Harvard University and earned a Ph.D. in Educational 
Administration from the Claremont Graduate University; professional: 
served as an elementary school teacher, acting principal, assistant 
superintendent of child welfare and attendance, and school psychologist; 
served on the faculty at both California State University, Los Angeles, 
and Long Beach; Health Occupations Specialist, California Department of 
Education; awards: named Legislator of the Year by numerous California 
universities, associations, and organizations; public service: Los 
Angeles Unified School District Board Member; served in the California 
State Senate for 20 years; served as U.S. Ambassador to Micronesia, 
1999-2001; elected to the 107th Congress, by special election, on June 
5, 2001.
