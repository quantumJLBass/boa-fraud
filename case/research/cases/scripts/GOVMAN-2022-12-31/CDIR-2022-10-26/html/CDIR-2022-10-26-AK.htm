                                 SENATORS

    LISA MURKOWSKI, Republican, of Anchorage, AK; born in Ketchikan, 
AK, May 22, 1957; education: Willamette University, 1975-77; Georgetown 
University, 1978-80, B.A., economics; Willamette College of Law, 1982-
85, J.D.; professional: attorney; private law practice; Alaska and 
Anchorage Bar Associations; public service: Anchorage Equal Rights 
Commission; Anchorage District Court Attorney, 1987-89; Task Force on 
the Homeless, 1990-91; Alaska State Representative, 1999-2002; family: 
married to Verne Martell; children: Nicholas and Matthew; committees: 
ranking member, Indian Affairs; Appropriations; Energy and Natural 
Resources; Health, Education, Labor, and Pensions; appointed to the U.S. 
Senate on December 20, 2002; elected to the U.S. Senate for a full term 
on November 2, 2004; reelected as a write-in candidate to the U.S. 
Senate on November 2, 2010; reelected to the U.S. Senate on November 8, 
2016.

    DAN SULLIVAN, Republican, of Anchorage, AK; born in Fairview Park, 
OH, November 13, 1964; education: B.A., economics, Harvard University, 
Cambridge, MA, 1987; J.D. and joint masters of science in foreign 
service, Georgetown University, Washington, DC, 1993; military: U.S. 
Marine Corps, 1993-97; U.S. Marine Corps Reserve, 1997-present, 
attaining rank of Colonel; professional: attorney, private law practice, 
1997-2001; public service: director, National Security Council Staff, 
2002-04; U.S. Assistant Secretary of State for Economic, Energy and 
Business Affairs, 2006-09; Attorney General, State of Alaska, 2009-10; 
commissioner, Alaska Department of Natural Resources, 2011-13; chairman, 
International Republican Institute (IRI), 2018-present; awards: National 
Security Council Outstanding Service Award; Defense Meritorious Service 
Medal; religion: Catholic; family: married to Julie Fate Sullivan; 
children: Meghan, Isabella, and Laurel; committees: Armed Services; 
Commerce, Science, and Transportation; Environment and Public Works; 
Veterans' Affairs; elected to the U.S. Senate on November 4, 2014; 
reelected to the U.S. Senate on November 3, 2020.

                              REPRESENTATIVE

                                 AT LARGE

    MARY SATTLER PELTOLA, Democrat, of Bethel, AK; born in Anchorage, 
AK, August 31, 1973; an enrolled member of the Orutsararmiut Traditional 
Native Council; education: attended the University of Northern Colorado, 
Greeley, Colo., 1991-94; attended the University of Alaska Fairbanks, 
Fairbanks, Alaska, 1994; attended the University of Alaska Southeast, 
Juneau, Alaska, 1995-97; attended the University of Alaska Anchorage, 
Anchorage, Alaska, 1997-98; professional: manager of community 
development and sustainability; professional advocate; executive 
director, Kuskokwim River inter-tribal fish commission, 2017-22; member 
of the Alaska State House of Representatives, 1999-09; member of the 
Bethel, Alaska, city council; committees: Education and Labor; Natural 
Resources; elected to the 117th Congress, by special election, on August 
16, 2022, to fill the vacancy created by the death of Congressman Don 
Young.
