    GEORGE V. VOINOVICH, Republican, of Cleveland, OH; born in 
Cleveland, on July 15, 1936; B.A., Ohio University, 1958; J.D., College 
of Law, Ohio State University, 1961; Honorary Doctorate of Law, Ohio 
University, 1981; Honorary Doctorate of Public Administration, Findlay 
University, 1993; public service: Assistant Attorney General, Ohio, 
1963; member, Ohio House of Representatives, 1967-71; Cuyahoga County 
Auditor, 1971-76; Cuyahoga County Commissioner, 1977-78; Lieutenant 
Governor, Ohio, 1979; Mayor, Cleveland, OH, 1979-86; 65th Governor of 
Ohio, 1990-98; President, National League of Cities, 1985; chairman, 
National Governor's Association, 1997-98; Catholic; married: Janet 
Voinovich; three children: George, Betsy, and Peter; committees: 
Governmental Affairs; Environment and Public Works; Select Committee on 
Ethics; elected to the U.S. Senate on November 3, 1998.
