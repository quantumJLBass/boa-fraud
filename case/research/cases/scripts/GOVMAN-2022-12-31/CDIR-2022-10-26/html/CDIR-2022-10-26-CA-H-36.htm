    RAUL RUIZ, M.D., Democrat, of Palm Springs, CA; born in Coachella, 
CA, August 25, 1972; education: B.S., University of California, Los 
Angeles, CA, 1994; M.D., Harvard University, Cambridge, MA, 2001; 
M.P.P., Harvard University, 2001; M.P.H., Harvard University, 2007; 
professional: emergency physician, Eisenhower Medical Center; founder, 
Coachella Valley Healthcare Initiative, 2010; senior associate dean, 
School of Medicine at University of California Riverside, 2011; 
caucuses: chair, Congressional Hispanic Caucus; co-chair, Congressional 
Tri-Caucus; vice chair, Congressional Diabetes Caucus; Aging Issues Task 
Force; ALS Caucus; Bipartisan Congressional Task Force on Alzheimer's 
Disease; California Democratic Congressional Delegation; Caucus for 
Advancement of Studio, Talent and Film Diversity; Climate Change Task 
Force; Congressional Academic Medicine Caucus; Congressional Animal 
Protection Caucus; Congressional Arthritis Caucus; Congressional Burn 
Pits Caucus; Congressional Caucus on Foster Youth; Congressional Caucus 
on Multicultural Media; Congressional Caucus to End the Youth Vaping 
Epidemic; Congressional Coronavirus Task Force; Congressional Cut Flower 
Caucus; Congressional Cystic Fibrosis Caucus; Congressional EMS Caucus; 
Congressional Heart and Stroke Coalition; Congressional HIV/AIDS Caucus; 
Congressional Iran Human Rights Democracy Caucus; Congressional Law 
Enforcement Caucus; Congressional LGBTQ+ Equality Caucus; Congressional 
Library of Congress Caucus; Congressional Native American Caucus; 
Congressional Primary Care Caucus; Congressional Public Broadcasting 
Caucus; Congressional Small Business Caucus; COVID-19 Task Force; Expand 
Social Security Caucus; Future Forum; House Democratic Caucus; House 
General Aviation Caucus; House Renewable Energy and Energy Efficiency 
Caucus; House Sustainable Energy and Environment Coalition; New Democrat 
Coalition; Pro-Choice Caucus; Quiet Skies Caucus; Seniors Task Force; 
Work for Warriors Caucus; committees: Energy and Commerce; Veterans' 
Affairs; elected to the 113th Congress on November 6, 2012; reelected to 
each succeeding Congress.
