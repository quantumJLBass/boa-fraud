    JASON T. SMITH, Republican, of Salem, MO; born in St. Louis, MO, 
June 16, 1980; education: graduate of Salem High School; received B.S. 
degrees, agricultural economics and business administration, with an 
emphasis in finance, University of Missouri, Columbia; earned law

[[Page 159]]

degree from Oklahoma City University School of Law; Trinity College, 
Cambridge, England; professional: attorney; real estate agent; small 
business owner and fourth generation owner of the family farm; NRA; 
Missouri Farm Bureau; former president, current member of the Salem FFA 
Alumni Association; holds an American FFA degree; elected to the 
Missouri State House of Representatives, 2005 (special election), 2006, 
and 2008; leadership: served as Majority Whip in the 96th General 
Assembly; youngest Speaker Pro Tem in the 97th General Assembly; 
religion: Assemblies of God; committees: ranking member, Budget; Ways 
and Means; elected, by special election, to the 113th Congress on June 
4, 2013, to fill the vacancy caused by the resignation of U.S. 
Representative Jo Ann Emerson; reelected to each succeeding Congress.
