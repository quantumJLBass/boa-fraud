    CORI BUSH, Democrat, of St. Louis, MO; born in St. Louis, July 21, 
1976; education: Cardinal Ritter College Prep High School, St. Louis, 
MO, 1994; attended Harris-Stowe State University, St. Louis, MO, 1995-
96; RN Diploma, Lutheran School of Nursing, St. Louis, MO, 2008; awards: 
2015 ``Woman of Courage'' Award from the Emmett Till Legacy Foundation, 
The Black Rep's 2020 Frankie Muse Freeman Spirit Award, International 
Nurses Association Top Nurse award, Jefferson City NAACP ``Trailblazer 
Award''; professional: nurse, pastor, community organizer; religion: 
Nondenominational Christian; parents: Errol Bush and Barbara Blakney; 
siblings: Kelli Bush and Perry Blakney; children: Angel and Zion; 
committees: Judiciary; Oversight and Reform; elected to the 117th 
Congress on November 3, 2020.
