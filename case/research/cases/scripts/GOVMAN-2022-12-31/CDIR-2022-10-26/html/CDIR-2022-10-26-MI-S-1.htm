    DEBBIE STABENOW, Democrat, of Lansing, MI; born in Gladwin, MI, 
April 29, 1950; education: Clare High School, Clare, MI; B.A., Michigan 
State University, East Lansing, MI, 1972; M.S.W., Michigan State 
University, 1975; public service: Commissioner, Ingham County, MI, 1975-
78, chairperson for 2 years; Michigan State House of Representatives, 
1979-90; Michigan State Senate, 1991-94; religion: Methodist; children: 
Todd and Michelle; committees: chair, Agriculture, Nutrition, and 
Forestry; Budget; Environment and Public Works; Finance; Joint Committee 
on Taxation; elected to the 105th Congress on November 5, 1996; 
reelected to the 106th Congress on November 3, 1998; elected to the U.S. 
Senate on November 7, 2000; reelected to each succeeding Senate term.
